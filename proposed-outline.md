# Blockchain & Mobile Technology
## 12-Week Course Outline

**Format:** Weeks 1–8 at 1 hour/week · Weeks 9–11 at 3 hours/week · Week 12 wrap-up
**Focus:** The evolution of the web, with deep emphasis on Web3 and Web4 (the agentic web), culminating in an agent-driven project build.

---

## Phase 1 — Foundations (Weeks 1–8, 1 hour each)

### Week 1 — The Evolution of the Web
The opening lecture. Trace the web through its eras and the defining components of each.

- **Web1 (read-only, ~1990s–2004):** static HTML pages, hyperlinks, directories/portals, dial-up, content consumption only, centralized publishing.
- **Web2 (read–write, ~2004–present):** dynamic content, social media, user-generated content, the cloud, APIs, mobile apps, platform giants, data centralization and the "you are the product" model.
- **Web3 (read–write–own, emerging):** decentralization, blockchain, tokens, wallets, ownership of data and assets, trustless systems, DAOs.
- **Web4 (read–write–own–act, AI-agent-driven):** autonomous AI agents, an intelligent and proactive web, agent-to-agent interaction, a machine-readable web, AI blended with decentralized infrastructure.
- Close with a comparison across eras and why the shift toward Web3/Web4 matters.

### Week 2 — Foundations of Web3
Why decentralization; the Web2 problems it aims to solve; distributed ledgers; peer-to-peer networks; trustlessness; and the conceptual pillars of decentralization, ownership, and transparency. Sets up everything that follows.

### Week 3 — Blockchain & Cryptography Core
How blockchains actually work: blocks, chains, hashing, public/private keys, digital signatures, immutability, and the double-spend problem.

### Week 4 — Consensus Mechanisms
Proof of Work, Proof of Stake, and alternatives; mining, validators, forks, and the energy and security trade-offs.

### Week 5 — Smart Contracts & Tokens
Ethereum, smart contracts, gas, fungible tokens vs. NFTs, DAOs, and a high-level look at Solidity.

### Week 6 — dApps & the Web3 Stack
Decentralized applications, wallets, front-end-to-blockchain connection, oracles, decentralized storage (IPFS), and how a Web3 app fits together.

### Week 7 — Web4: The Agentic Web
AI agents as actors on the web, agent-to-agent communication, autonomous transactions, the convergence of AI and blockchain (agents using wallets and smart contracts), the machine-readable web, and emerging standards.

### Week 8 — Challenges, Trends & Ethics
Scalability, regulation, privacy, security risks, energy, and AI-agent accountability and trust. Introduce the project here.

---

## Phase 2 — Applied & Project Work (Weeks 9–11, 3 hours each)

### Week 9 — Hands-on Development I (Fundamentals)
Set up the dev environment, create a wallet, connect to a testnet, and write and deploy a simple smart contract manually — so students understand what is happening under the hood before adding agents.

### Week 10 — Agent-Driven dApp Development
Introduce the agentic / AI-assisted programming approach to building dApps faster: prompting for code, agent-assisted smart contract and front-end development, reviewing and debugging AI-generated code, and connecting the pieces into a working dApp. Students build a dApp using this workflow.

### Week 11 — Full Agentic Project Build
A complete project end-to-end using the agentic approach: planning, building, and integrating wallet, contract, and UI (and optionally autonomous agent components), with guided troubleshooting. The main project sprint.

---

## Phase 3 — Wrap-up (Week 12)

### Week 12 — Project Presentations & Review
Student demos, peer feedback, course recap, and discussion of career and research directions.

---

### Progression at a glance
Build it by hand (Week 9) → build it with agents (Week 10) → full agentic project (Week 11) → present (Week 12).
